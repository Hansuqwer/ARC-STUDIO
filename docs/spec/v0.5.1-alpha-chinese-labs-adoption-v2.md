## 6. Decision log

**Why script-then-review instead of fully automated import:**

1. **CoSAI compliance is by review.** A human looking at the rendered Python before commit is the policy enforcement; automation that bypasses review weakens the audit trail.
2. **Addendum has policy-only info** (deprecation dates, auto-routes, free-tier exceptions) that models.dev doesn't track. Manual overlay is unavoidable.
3. **Cross-check warnings are actionable.** If models.dev says GPT-5.5 input is $5/M and the addendum says $4.50/M (because a price change happened in between), the warning surfaces the discrepancy; reviewer decides which is current.
4. **Per-vendor commits are reviewable diffs.** A single `git diff` showing one vendor's rows is auditable; a 40-row mega-commit is not.

**Why not depend on models.dev at runtime:**

That's the v0.7-alpha-opt-in-cloud-features.md scope. Different threat model (third-party uptime, signature handling, consent). v0.5.1 stays purely local-first by limiting models.dev contact to dev-time-only.
